Phillip Hughes Tribute Page

Introduction

Phillip Hughes was an Australian cricketer who played for the national team as well as for domestic teams such as New South Wales and South Australia. He was born on November 30, 1988 in Macksville, New South Wales and started playing cricket at a young age. Hughes made his first-class debut for New South Wales in 2007 and soon became a regular in the team. He was known for his attacking batting style and his ability to score runs quickly. Tragically, Hughes passed away in November 2014 after being struck by a bouncer during a Sheffield Shield match at the Sydney Cricket Ground.

Early Life

Phillip Hughes was born in Macksville, a small town on the north coast of New South Wales. His father Greg was a banana farmer and cricket enthusiast who introduced his son to the sport at a young age. Hughes started playing cricket for Macksville at the age of six and quickly showed his talent. He was soon playing for representative teams and catching the eye of talent scouts.

In 2001, Hughes moved to Sydney to attend the prestigious cricket school Westfields Sports High School. There, he received top-class coaching and was able to develop his skills as a batsman. Hughes also played for the Western Suburbs Cricket Club in Sydney's grade cricket competition, where he was able to play against experienced players and hone his technique.

Career

Hughes made his first-class debut for New South Wales in 2007 at the age of 18. He scored a half-century in his second match and soon became a regular in the team. In 2009, Hughes was called up to the Australian national team for a Test series against South Africa. He scored a century in his second Test match and became the youngest player to score back-to-back centuries in Test cricket.

Despite his early success, Hughes had a somewhat stop-start international career. He was in and out of the team and struggled for consistency at times. However, he continued to score runs for New South Wales and was a highly-regarded player in domestic cricket.

Tragically, Hughes passed away in November 2014 after being struck by a bouncer during a Sheffield Shield match at the Sydney Cricket Ground. He was just 25 years old.
